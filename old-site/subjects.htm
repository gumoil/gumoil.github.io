A wide range of subjects appeals to me and this has taken me to many places. My first love is magnificent individual trees: the Beeches, Oaks and Chestnuts of Ireland and the United Kingdom.

A second obsession has been with graveyard architecture in this country and, especially, Europe.

I am also drawn to architectural images ranging from modest homes to skyscrapers and from aging factories to the early grain elevators of the American midwest.

In recent years I spent considerable energy on a photographic survey of those structures which remain in the Nazi concentration camps and printing them in gumoil. This project involved three trips to Europe, six countries, and many hours of cold weather walking through the ten camps in which buildings have been preserved. Sixty finished pictures came from this effort. Several will be exhibited at the Museum of the Holocaust in conjunction with the City of Houston's Fotofest in March, 2004.
