The concept of Polychromatic Gumoil Photographic Printing came to me in 1990 while I was learning traditional 19th Century gum bichromate methodology. I tried the new idea and it worked. I gradually reduced my private practice (clinical psychology) and shifted to teaching, writing, and exhibiting work in this wonderful art medium.

The images reproduced here suffer some from down-sizing and digitizing (I usually print in 11"x 17" or larger) and the inevitable loss of surface texture. The originals are created on heavy water color papers whose fabric contributes to the final result.

No two prints are quite the same although some come close; there are too many variables to achieve true replication. Consequently, each print is unique and not editioned.
